📊 REACTION — Key Drivers of the Economy & Stock Market

🇷 R — Rates

  • Fed Funds Rate direction
  • Yield Curve (e.g., 2s10s, 3m10y inversion)
  • Liquidity conditions
  • Global central bank alignment
  • Lending rates: mortgages, credit spreads

📈 E — Earnings

  • S&P 500 earnings growth trends
  • Corporate profit margins & guidance
  • Sector-level leadership (e.g., tech, energy)
  • EPS revisions (up/down)
  • Capex, innovation, AI spending boosts

🧠 A — Attitudes

  • Consumer sentiment (U. of Mich., Conf. Board)
  • CEO Confidence Index
  • Investor psychology (greed/fear)
  • Institutional vs retail positioning
  • BofA Fund Manager Survey, VIX levels

⚔️ C — Conflict (War, Geopolitics)

  • Global tensions (Ukraine, Taiwan, Middle East)
  • Military spending trends
  • Commodity disruptions (oil, semis, ag)
  • Trade and cyber warfare concerns
  • Red Sea/South China Sea shipping risks

💸 T — Treasury, Deficit, Debt

  • U.S. debt-to-GDP ratio (122%+)
  • Treasury auctions: bid-to-cover, issuance pressure
  • Rising interest expense burden
  • Fiscal policy direction (e.g., CHIPS Act, bailouts)
  • Long-term solvency and credibility

🔥 I — Inflation

  • Core CPI, Core PCE trends
  • Wage growth, service vs goods inflation
  • Rent/shelter costs
  • Global inflation comparisons (Eurozone, EM)
  • Sticky vs flexible inflation components

🔮 O — Outlook

  • Fed and IMF economic forecasts
  • Street consensus: soft landing vs hard
  • Productivity boosts via tech (AI, reshoring)
  • Recession models, yield curve signals
  • Expectations for GDP, earnings, employment

🛡️ N — National Security, Natural Disasters, Narratives

  • Cybersecurity and defense realignment
  • Weather, drought, earthquake disruptions
  • Dominant macro narratives (AI boom, energy policy)
  • Black Swan risks and insurance markets

Use this mnemonic to structure macro commentary, investment outlooks, or strategic portfolio reviews.
